## Scope and Expectations

This is not a ‚Äúone and done‚Äù design task. You are expected to:

- Explore multiple concepts before settling on a final direction
- Justify your design decisions using references and constraints
- Consider how items will be used in practice, for example events, launches, daily use, or outreach

- The project will not include production/printing, but your work should be detailed enough that the team could confidently proceed to manufacturing.

## Suggested Workflow

You may structure your work roughly as follows:

### 1. Research and Inspiration

- Review PAST‚Äôs brand guidelines
- Collect examples of merch from:
  - University teams
  - Space, aerospace, and engineering organisations
  - Student societies and research groups
- Identify what works well and what does not

### 2. Concept Development

- Select 1‚Äì3 merch items to focus on
- Produce rough sketches or digital mockups
- Explore variations in colour, layout, and placement
- Pay special attention to mission patches if you choose to work on them

### 3. Feedback and Iteration

- Share concepts with other recruits and team members
- Collect structured feedback
- Refine designs based on this feedback

### 4. Final Design

- Prepare final designs in appropriate vector formats
- Ensure files are suitable for printing or embroidery
- Include clear notes on colours, dimensions, and materials where relevant

### 5. Supplier Investigation

- Research manufacturers or suppliers
- Compare:
  - Pricing and minimum order quantities
  - Lead times
  - Customisation options
  - Location and shipping considerations
- Summarise pros and cons of each option

### 6. Documentation

- Compile your work into a short report or shared document
- Include:
  - Inspiration and references
  - Design rationale
  - Final designs
  - Supplier research and recommendations


## Deliverables

At minimum, your documentation should include:
- A small collection of inspiration references
- Final design files in a suitable format
- Mockups showing how the merch would look in use
- A short document summarising your process and findings
- Clear communication is just as important as strong visuals.
